Module 1 of 5 — Root Cause
Why this objection happens
Most small and mid-size MSPs are not actively monitoring processor statements. They set up a gateway years ago and auto-pay the bill without scrutinizing line items.

Effective rates, gateway markups, and per-transaction fees blend into one monthly number. The prospect genuinely does not know their real cost because no one has ever broken it down for them.

The broken rep instinct

When a prospect says they don't know what they're paying, most reps hear an opening and immediately pivot to the savings pitch: "We typically save MSPs 20–30%..."

Why this fails: A percentage savings claim requires a baseline. If the prospect has no baseline, the claim is meaningless. You are asking them to trust a math problem they cannot verify. The conversation stalls or ends.

The right frame

Stop selling savings. Start offering clarity. The prospect cannot make a buying decision without a number. Your job is to help them get that number first, before any comparison to AP's pricing.

❌ Don't do this

  • Continue the "we save you X%" framing with no baseline
  • Guess their current rate or fabricate a comparison
  • Skip past the objection and jump to a product demo

✅ Do this instead

  • Acknowledge that not knowing is normal and common
  • Pivot the CTA from demo to free fee audit
  • Make the audit zero-commitment and low-friction

Module 2 of 5 — The Script
Word-for-word script
This is the scripted response to use when a prospect surfaces this objection. Learn it in sequence. Each line has a purpose.

Goal: Reframe the call. Stop selling savings. Start offering clarity. The free fee audit becomes the next step, replacing the demo as the immediate CTA.

Prospect
"Honestly, I'm not sure what I'm paying in fees. We've had the same processor for years."
Rep — Line 1 (normalize)
"That's actually the most common thing we hear. Most MSPs are paying more than they realize because the fees are spread across a platform bill, a gateway bill, and a processor bill, and none of those statements use the same line items. You'd need to add all three together to get your real cost."
Rep — Line 2 (pivot to audit)
"Here's what I'd suggest: before we talk about what you could save, let me show you what you're actually spending. We can do a free fee audit, you send me your last two or three processor statements, and I'll put together a clear breakdown of your effective rate, your fixed costs, and where the money is going. No strings, no pitch deck. Just a clean number."
Rep — Line 3 (close)
"Then, whether you move forward with us or not, at least you'll know exactly what your current setup is costing you. Does that sound useful?"

Why each line works

Line 1
Normalizes the situation. The prospect stops feeling embarrassed or behind. Removes defensiveness.
Line 2
Pivots the CTA from a demo (high-friction, requires trust) to an audit (low-friction, pure value). "No strings" removes the sales pressure explicitly.
Line 3
"Whether you move forward with us or not" is not a throwaway line. It signals confidence and de-risks the ask. The closed question "Does that sound useful?" gets a yes with no commitment attached.

Module 3 of 5 — The Fee Audit
Running the fee audit
The audit is the deliverable that earns the next conversation. Know exactly what to ask for and how to calculate the number.

What to request (2–3 months)

  • Processor / merchant account statement
    Shows effective rate, per-transaction fees, chargeback fees. Often the largest bill.
  • Gateway statement
    e.g., BNG Gateway, Stripe, Square, QuickBooks Payments. A separate line item most prospects don't track.
  • Platform / SaaS bill
    e.g., ConnectBooster, FlexPoint, WisePay. The "wrapper" fee on top of processing.
  • Bank transfer / ACH fees
    Some banks charge per-transfer fees that don't appear on the processor statement.

Competitor flags during the audit

ConnectBooster
Look for 3 separate bills: CB SaaS + BNG Gateway + Clearent processing. Prospect who thinks they pay "one fee" is almost certainly underestimating total cost.
WisePay
Confirm if they've been hit by the Global Payments rate increase to 3.5%. Many WisePay users don't know this has passed through to their statement.
FlexPoint
Ask about card mix. Interchange-plus means effective rate fluctuates. A prospect paying "2.5%" may actually be at 3.5%+ when clients use premium rewards cards.
QB Payments
Charges 2.5–3.5% per transaction with no flat-rate option. No PSA integration either, which often surfaces a second pain point during the audit.

"Would your accountant be able to pull the last few statements so we can take a look together? This is usually a twenty-minute task." Do not disqualify — involve the accountant.

How to route it
Bank portals have opaque pricing and no PSA integration. The audit is still valuable. Lead with the fee transparency angle, then surface the workflow inefficiency as a second pain point.
